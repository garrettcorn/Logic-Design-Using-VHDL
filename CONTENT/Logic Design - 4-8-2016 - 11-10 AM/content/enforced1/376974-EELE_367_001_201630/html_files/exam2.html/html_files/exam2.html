Exam Details

12.5% of your grade (125 points) is from an exam covering modules 10, 11, and 12.  The exam is in the form of a multiple choice quiz within D2L.  Once you begin the exam, you will have 1 hour to complete it.  Once you start the exam, there is no stopping the timer and you only get one attempt.  So make sure you block out a 1 hour uninterrupted period.   

How to Study

The exam is designed so that you don't have enough time to look up every single question in the book or in your past homework/quizzes.  This means you need to study so that you can quickly answer most questions (~75%) and only need to look up a few questions (~25%).  I recommend allocating 8 hours of studying for this exam.  It is worth 12.5% of your grade so it shouldn't be taken lightly.  Study 2-3 hours a day for a few days leading up to the exam.  A good way to study is to read the chapter summaries in the text book and then read the exercise problem description for every problem.  For each problem, ask your self if you understand what is being asked and if you could either do the problem quickly or know where to look to find out the answer or how to solve the problem.  If you don't know how to do a problem, take the time to review how to solve it by either reading the textbook, going back to the lecture videos, or looking at past homework/quizzes.
